## Interview Overview

**Unschool Discoveries** is a story-world for curious families. It opens the door to conversations about books, creativity, unschooling, real-life learning, and why children deserve stories that reflect the way they truly live and learn.

## Interview Topics
   

### Why did you create Unschool Discoveries?

Many children’s books still treat school as the center of childhood. **Unschool Discoveries** was created to show something different: children learning through life, play, questions, and adventure.

### What is Unschool Discoveries?

**Unschool Discoveries** is a story-world project made for curious families and self-directed learners. It includes books and connected media that celebrate wonder, freedom, and learning through real life.

### Why does this kind of representation matter?

Children need stories where they can see themselves. Families who live and learn differently should not feel left out. They deserve books that feel familiar, welcoming, and true to their lives.

### How is this different from other educational books?

Many books try to teach by feeling like school. **Unschool Discoveries** does something else. It shows learning as part of everyday life: exploring, noticing, asking, making, and trying new things.

### What does “learning through living” mean?

It means learning happens all the time. Children learn when they play, talk, build, travel, wonder, read, help, and explore the world around them.

### Why use stories to talk about learning?

Stories help people feel ideas, not just hear them. A story can help a child feel seen. It can also help adults understand a different way of learning in a deeper and more human way.

### What is the difference between Unschool Discoveries and Unschool Pages?

**Unschool Discoveries** is the story-world front door. It is where readers enter through the books and the main public site.

**Unschool Pages** is the connected living publication platform. It goes deeper with notes, rabbit holes, behind-the-scenes posts, and expanded ideas from the world.

### Why build a whole universe instead of just one book?

Because the idea is bigger than one title. The goal is to build a lasting world where curiosity, freedom, and real-life learning can keep growing through more stories, pages, and discoveries.

### What kinds of conversations do you hope this project starts?

I hope it starts conversations about childhood, trust, freedom, creativity, curiosity, and what learning can look like outside of school-centered thinking.

### Who is this project for?

It is for unschooling families, self-directed learners, curious readers, and anyone who wants stories filled with wonder, discovery, and real-life learning.

## Sample Interview Questions

- What inspired you to create **Unschool Discoveries**?
- What gap did you see in children’s books?
- Why was it important to make stories for unschoolers?
- What does learning through living mean to you?
- What do you hope children feel when they read these books?
- What do you hope parents feel?
- How does **Unschool Pages** grow the world even more?
- Why does worldbuilding matter for this project?
- What do you hope this work adds to the way people think about childhood and learning?
